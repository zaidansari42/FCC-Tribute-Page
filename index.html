Jeremy Clarkson

The man who made cars entertating.

Timeline of Jeremy's Life

  • 1960 - Born in Doncaster, England
  • 1984 - Started his writing career from writing Amstard Computer User.
  • 1988 - His first major television role came as one of the presenters on the British motoring programme Top Gear, from 27 October 1988.
  • 2000 - He presented the first series UK version of Robot Wars.His talk show, Clarkson, comprised 27 half-hour episodes aired in the United Kingdom, and featured guest interviews with musicians, politicians and television personalities.
  • 2002 - He was credited with turning Top Gear into the most-watched TV show on BBC Two, rebroadcast to over 100 countries around the world.
  • 2004 - For an episode of the first series of the BBC's Who Do You Think You Are? broadcast in November, Clarkson was invited to investigate his family history. It included the story of his great-great-great grandfather, John Kilner, who invented the Kilner jar, a container for preserved fruit.
  • 2007 - Won the National Television Awards Special Recognition Award
  • 2008 - Sustained minor injuries to his legs, back and hand in an intentional collision with a brick wall while making the 12th series of Top Gear
  • 2014 - Received a £4.8 million dividend and an £8.4 million share buyout from BBC Worldwide, bringing his estimated income for the year to more than £14 million.
  • 2015 - It was announced that Clarkson, along with former Top Gear hosts Richard Hammond and James May would present a new show on Amazon Prime Video. Clarkson confirmed on his Twitter feed that the series would be titled The Grand Tour, and air from a different location each week.
  • 2018 - It was announced that Clarkson would host a revamped series of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?
